# Gather Round Physical Die Spec

## Prototype Direction

- Product: oversized foam dinner die
- Size: 3 inches per side
- Material: soft foam, safe to toss indoors
- Audience: Christian families with young kids
- Visual tone: playful but polished
- Gameplay: all six sides are playable

## Faces

1. Heart
   - Theme: feelings, gratitude, honesty, worries, joy
   - Icon direction: heart

2. Faith
   - Theme: God, prayer, Jesus, Scripture, spiritual growth
   - Icon direction: cross, candle, light, or open Bible

3. Family
   - Theme: home, encouragement, memories, forgiveness, helping
   - Icon direction: house, table, or simple family mark

4. Friends
   - Theme: kindness, courage, school, conflict, neighbors
   - Icon direction: two people, handshake, or smile pair

5. Tomorrow
   - Theme: next steps, habits, wise choices, service
   - Icon direction: sunrise, arrow, path, or seedling

6. Wild
   - Theme: silly dinner-table challenges
   - Icon direction: the QR code itself, styled as the hero graphic

## QR Face Guidance

- Keep the QR code high contrast and large.
- Decorate around the QR code more than inside it.
- Avoid placing key QR modules near rounded foam edges.
- Use matte printing if possible to reduce glare.
- Include a short URL on packaging as a backup.
- Test scans from multiple phones before approving production.

## Manufacturing Path

1. Make flat artwork for all six faces.
2. Generate a real QR code once the website URL is known.
3. Order one or two sample foam cubes from a promotional-product or custom-toy supplier.
4. Scan-test the QR side under kitchen lighting, at angles, and after light handling.
5. Toss-test indoors for safety, durability, and ink wear.
6. Revise artwork based on sample quality.
7. Request quotes for small batch quantities before committing to tooling or inventory.

## Open Questions

- Final logo and icon system
- Final QR destination URL
- Whether to use one color per face or a unified cream-base die with colored artwork
- Packaging format: box, pouch, or belly band
- Whether the first production run should include a table card with the short URL
